# Coty subsidiary files lawsuit against Kering over Gucci licence after L’Oréal deal

 By Lynsey Barber | Published: 11-Nov-2025  

[Regulatory](/tag/regulatory) [Finance](/tag/finance)

Kering recently signed a new deal with L’Oréal to create fragrance and beauty lines for Gucci after its current partnership with Coty ends

A subsidiary of [Coty](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/coty-sales-sink-as-prestige-and-consumer-lag) has filed a lawsuit against [Gucci](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/gucci-beauty-names-zhang-linghe-fragrance-ambassador) and its owner [Kering](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/how-i-became-managing-director-of-kering-beaut%C3%A9-uk-ireland).

Coty currently [holds the licence](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/coty-share-price-surges-potential-sale-report) to produce Gucci’s beauty and fragrance lines, which includes scents such as [Gucci Guilty](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/a-ap-rocky-julia-garner-and-elliot-page-are-the-new-faces-of-gucci-guilty-207059) and [Gucci Bloom](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/gucci-bloom-creates-a-floral-paradise-in-harrods-132303).

[Kering struck a new deal with L’Oréal in October](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/kering-offloads-beauty-division-to-l-or%C3%A9al-in-historic) that includes the rights to enter into a 50-year exclusive license for the creation, development, and distribution of fragrance and beauty products for [Gucci](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/kering-owned-gucci-balenciaga-and-mcqueen-hit-by).

This will commence after the expiration of the current license with [Coty](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/l-or%C3%A9al-is-fueling-the-future-of-ai), Kering stated at the time the [L’Oréal](https://cosmeticsbusiness.com/l-or%C3%A9al-paris-dethrones-charlotte-tilbury-as-uk-s-top) deal was announced.
